DEFINITIONS

1. QUALITY – Degree to which a set of inherent characteristics of an object fulfills requirements.
2. QUALITY POLICY – Policy (intentions and direction of an organization as formally expressed by its top management) related to Quality.
3. QUALITY OBJECTIVE – Objective (result to be achieved) related to Quality.
4. QUALITY ASSURANCE – Part of Quality Management focused on providing confidence that quality requirements (need or expectation that is stated, generally implied or obligatory) will be fulfilled.
5. QUALITY CONTROL – Part of quality management focused on fulfilling quality requirements.
6. QUALITY MANUAL – Specification (document stating requirements) for the Quality Management System of an organization.
7. QUALITY PLAN – Specification of the procedures (specified way to carried out and activity or a process) and associated resources to be applied when and whom to a specific object.
8. MANAGEMENT – Coordinated activities to direct and control an organization.
9. ORGANIZATION – Person or group of people that has its own functions with responsibilities, authorities and relationships to achieve its objectives.
10. QUALITY MANAGEMENT SYSTEM – Part of a management system (set of interrelated or interacting elements of an organization to establish policies and objectives, and processes to achieve those objectives) with regard to Quality.
11. AUDIT – Systematic, Independent and Documented process for obtaining objective evidence and evaluating it objectively to determine the extent to which audit criteria are fulfilled. 12. CORRECTION – Action to eliminate a detected nonconformity.
13. CORRECTIVE ACTION – Action to eliminate the cause of a nonconformity and to prevent recurrence.
14. PREVENTIVE ACTION – Action to eliminate the cause of a potential nonconformity or other potential undesirable situation.
15. NONCONFORMITY – Non-fulfillment of requirement.
16. PROCESS – Set of interrelated and interacting activities that use inputs to deliver an intended result.
17. SYSTEM – Set of interrelated and interacting elements.
18. IMPROVEMENT – Activity to enhance performance.
19. CONTINUAL IMPROVEMENT – Recurring activity to enhance performance.
20. DOCUMENTED INFORMATION – Information required to be controlled and maintained by an organization and the medium on which it is contained.
21. DESIGN AND DEVELOPMENT – Set of process that transform requirements for an object into more detail requirements for that project.
22. AUDIT SCOPE – Extent and boundaries of an audit.
23. AUDIT PROGRAMME – Set of one or more audits planned for a specific time frame and directed towards a specific purpose.
24. AUDIT PLAN – Description of the activities and arrangement for an audit.
25. AUDIT CRITERIA – Set of policies, procedures or requirements used as a reference against which objective evidence is compared.
26. STRATEGY – Plan to achieve long term or overall objectives.
27. VISION – Aspiration of what an organization would like to become as expressed by top management.
28. REQUIREMENT – Need or expectation that is stated, generally implied or obligatory.
29. STATUTORY REQUIREMENT – Obligatory requirement specified by a legislative body.
30. REGULATORY REQUIREMENT – Obligatory requirement specified by an authority mandated by a legislative body.
31. RISK – Effect of uncertainty.
32. RISK MANAGEMENT – Coordinated activities to direct and control risks of an organization.
33. RISK AND OPPORTUNITY – Potential adverse effect (threats) and potential beneficial effects (opportunities).
34. VERIFICATION – Confirmation, through the provision of objective evidence, that specified requirements have been fulfilled.
35. VALIDATION – Confirmation, through the provision of objective evidence, that specified requirements for a specific intended use or application have been fulfilled.
36. TECHNICAL EXPERT – Person who provides specific knowledge or expertise to the audit team.

PROJECT BACKGROUND & SCOPE

ZAKUM Field is located in the central part of the Arabian Gulf at about 65 KM NW of Abu Dhabi town and 100 KM to the East- Southeast of Das Island. Lower Zakum field Facilities include approx. 118 wellhead towers hosting oil producers, gas injectors and water injectors

The crude oil produced from the Lower Zakum wells is collected from the production sites to two main offshore gathering and primary processing centres, Zakum Central Super Complex (ZCSC) and Zakum Western Super Complex (ZWSC) via a field network of around 310 KM length including the 24” line between ZWSC and ZCSC

The oil processed at ZCSC is routed to ZWSC via 24” line for onward transfer. The combined field oil production from ZWSC/ZCSC is then routed through the 30” MOL (92KM) to Das Island where final processing, stabilization, storage and offloading to tankers is done

Lower Zakum LTDP1 will be implemented in phases. The LZ LTDP (Phase-1), which is the focus of this EPC, comprise building up and sustaining the plateau rate of 450 MBD upto 2035. To further extend the plateau beyond 2035 will necessitate additional facilities which will be part of future phased development LZ LTDP (Phase- 2 & 3)

The overall Scope of WORK includes, but not limited to, the Engineering, Procurement, Construction, Installation and Commissioning of all the new facilities and main components of 2 main Oil Trains as summarized ::

  • 1. Separation Trains: 150 MSTBOPD two (2) trains having HP Separator, Oil / Oil exchanger, LP Separator & Crude Oil Charge Pumps.
  • 2. Desalting / Dehydrator: 200 MSTBOPD two (2) Trains
  • 3. LP oil stabilization system: 200 MSTBOPD two (2) trains having Oil stabilizer column, Oil stabilizer bottom pumps, Oil stabilizer side reboiler, Oil stabilizer column reboiler, and Product oil / oil exchanger.

8.0 AUDIT FOLLOW UP AND CLOSEOUT

Audit Findings and reports shall be issued to all interested parties within 07 Working days of the formal audit close out meeting. If it is delayed, the reasons should be communicated to the auditee and the individual(s) managing the audit program. Whenever the audit report is issued, the audit findings shall be recorded in the audit log sheet for the traceability until the findings are closed. The auditors, Project Quality Lead, shall trace the corrective actions corresponding to the applicable audit findings. As per A0-Q-PQ-SP-002(Rev.0) Specification for Requirements for Projects Contractor Quality System, Clause 3.7.1, Paragraph No.: 09, All non-conformances shall be closed within six (6) weeks of identification, or the closeout date must be approved by COMPANY.

On satisfactory completion of corrective actions, Auditee shall provide evidence to the Auditor to close out the agreed action. Upon satisfactory review of close out evidence, Auditor will close the Audit findings, and the Auditee will be notified.

OBJECTIVES OF RISK MANAGEMENT

Identify Risks:

The primary objective of project risk management is to identify potential risks that could impact the project's objectives, deliverables, schedule, budget, or quality. This involves systematically identifying and documenting risks throughout the project lifecycle, considering both internal and external factors.

Assess Risks:

Project risk management aims to assess the likelihood and potential impact of identified risks on the project's success criteria. This involves evaluating risks qualitatively or quantitatively to prioritize them based on their significance and develop appropriate response strategies.

Mitigate Risks:

The objective of project risk management is to develop and implement risk response strategies to mitigate or reduce the likelihood or impact of identified risks. This may involve avoiding, transferring, mitigating, or accepting risks based on their severity, urgency, and feasibility of response measures.

Monitor Risks:

Project risk management aims to monitor and control risks throughout the project lifecycle to ensure that risk response measures are effective and timely. This involves tracking key risk indicators, monitoring changes in the risk landscape and taking corrective actions as necessary to address emerging risks.

Optimize Opportunities:

In addition to managing threats, project risk management seeks to identify and capitalize on opportunities that may arise during the project lifecycle. This involves identifying positive risks or opportunities that could enhance project outcomes and developing strategies to exploit them for the project's benefit.

Ensure Project Success:

The overarching objective of project risk management is to enhance the likelihood of project success by effectively managing risks that could impact project objectives, deliverables, or stakeholder expectations. This involves minimizing the impact of negative risks while maximizing the benefits of positive risks.
